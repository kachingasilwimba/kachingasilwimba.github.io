Projects

Assessing the Impact of Soil Parameters on Soil Moisture Dynamics in CLMS Across the Contiguous United States Using Empirical Orthogonal Function Analysis

Objective: Evaluate how different soil parameters influence soil moisture dynamics in the Community Land Model (CLMS) across the Contiguous United States (CONUS) to improve model calibration and land management decisions.

Methods: Utilized Empirical Orthogonal Function (EOF) analysis to decompose data into spatial and temporal components, identifying dominant patterns and variability in soil moisture.

Outcomes: Published work showing how soil parameter setups affect soil moisture patterns, leading to more accurate predictions and better land management decisions.

Enhancing Soil Moisture Pattern Recognition in CLMS Simulations Over CONUS by Integrating Empirical Orthogonal Functions with Self-Organizing Maps

Objective: Improve recognition and understanding of soil moisture patterns in CLMS simulations by combining EOF with Self-Organizing Maps (SOM) for detailed spatial and temporal analysis.

Methods: Integrated EOF and SOM to cluster data and identify patterns in soil moisture from CLMS simulations.

Outcomes: Contributed to a 30% improvement in validation metrics, enhancing accuracy and interpretability of soil moisture dynamics.

Emulating and Quantifying Uncertainty in CLMS Simulations Using Evidential Deep Neural Networks and Augmented Perturbed Parameter Ensembles

Objective: Emulate and quantify uncertainty in CLMS simulations to improve reliability and trustworthiness of model outputs, reducing computational costs.

Methods: Employed Evidential Deep Neural Networks (EDNN) and Augmented Perturbed Parameter Ensembles to analyze simulations and estimate uncertainty.

Outcomes: Under review; presented at conferences, showing promise in delivering enhanced uncertainty estimates.

Advancing Prediction and Uncertainty Estimation in Ungauged Basins Using Evidential Deep Learning Frameworks

Objective: Develop robust models for hydrological forecasting in data-scarce regions using advanced machine learning techniques.

Methods: Utilized Evidential Deep Learning Frameworks to handle uncertainty in predictions for ungauged basins.

Outcomes: In preparation; part of ongoing research to address challenges in hydrological modeling.
